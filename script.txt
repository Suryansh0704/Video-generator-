I was there, a shadow amongst the STONE WALLS of the Tower of London, when two young princes vanished. Their GHOSTS seemed to linger, a haunting aura whispering of foul play. The year 1483 was a tumultuous time, their disappearance a political earthquake.

Edward V and his younger brother, Richard, Duke of York, were meant to be safe. Their chambers became a tomb; their innocence cooked by raw power. I watched as their uncle, Richard III, ascended the THRONE, a crown heavy with suspicion, leaving countless questions.

The SERVANTS whispered of secret passages, of hurried footsteps. The plot was thick. The truth obscured by fear and loyalty; I knew the answer, an unspeakable act of ambition sealing their tragic FATE. That truth, deep beneath the castle, always makes me wonder who truly holds the power within those stone walls.
